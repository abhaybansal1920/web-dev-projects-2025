About the Movie

Interstellar is set in a future where Earth is slowly dying because of dust storms and crop failure. A group of astronauts travel through a wormhole near Saturn to search for a new planet where humans can survive.

I like this movie because it mixes real science (like black holes and time dilation) with emotions, family and sacrifice. I first watched it in school and then rewatched it in college. The background music and space visuals make it feel very grand and emotional.

  • Release Year: 2014

Storyline (Short Summary)

Earth is facing a serious food crisis because crops are failing and dust storms are common. Cooper is secretly contacted by NASA and asked to pilot a mission through a wormhole to find habitable planets in another galaxy.

The team visits different planets, each with extreme conditions. Due to the strong gravity near the black hole, time runs slower for them than on Earth. This means that a few hours for them can be many years on Earth, which creates emotional distance between Cooper and his family.

In the end, the movie talks about the importance of love, human connection and hope, along with scientific ideas like gravity and dimensions beyond the normal three we see.

Fun Trivia

Some interesting facts about Interstellar:

  • The appearance of the black hole "Gargantua" in the movie is based on actual physics equations from physicist Kip Thorne.
  • Time moves slower near the black hole. In the water planet scene, one hour equals about seven years on Earth (time dilation).
  • The background music (especially the organ sounds) was used to create a feeling of emptiness, mystery and spirituality in space.
  • The robot TARS looks very simple in shape but is different from the usual humanoid robots shown in most sci-fi movies.
